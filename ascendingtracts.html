Ascending Tracts of the Spinal Cord

Definition & Purpose

Sensory nerve fibers entering the spinal cord are sorted and segregated into **nerve bundles or tracts** in the white matter, linking different segments or ascending to higher centers (brain).

Function:

Conduct **afferent information**, which may or may not reach the level of **consciousness**.

Types of Information Conveyed

  • Exteroceptive: Information originating from **outside** the body (Pain, Temperature, Crude Touch).
  • Proprioceptive: Information originating from **inside** the body (Muscles, Joints, Position, Movement).

Neuron Pathway Breakdown

  • First-order neuron:

    • Cell Body Posterior root ganglion.
    • Connection Peripheral process connects to sensory receptor.
    • Input Central process enters posterior root to synapse.

  • Second-order neuron:

    • Decussation Axon crosses obliquely in the spinal cord.
    • Ascent Ascends to synapse on the third-order neuron in the **thalamus**.

  • Third-order neuron:

    • Cell Body Thalamus.
    • Projection Projects via projection fiber to the **somatosensory cortex** (postcentral gyrus).

Branching & Reflexes

  • Reticular Formation: Many neurons branch here to activate the cerebral cortex, helping maintain **wakefulness**.
  • Motor Neurons: Other branches synapse on motor neurons to initiate **reflex muscular activity**.

Key Features

  • Receptors: Free nerve endings in skin and tissues.
  • Fiber Types: Delta A-type (**fast**) ‚Üí sharp/acute pain; C-type (**slow**) ‚Üí burning/aching pain.
  • Somatotopic Order: Sacral fibers are **lateral**, cervical fibers are **medial**.

Flow of Signals (The Pathway)

  • 1st Order Enter posterior root ganglion ‚Üí tip of posterior gray column ‚Üí **Posterolateral Tract of Lissauer**.
  • Synapse Synapse in **Substantia Gelatinosa** (Rexed Lamina II). **Substance P** is released here.
  • 2nd Order Axons cross obliquely to the opposite side and ascend in the **Lateral Spinothalamic Tract**.
  • Ascent Ascends through Medulla (lateral surface), Pons, and Midbrain.
  • Spinal Lemniscus Joins Anterior Spinothalamic and Spinotectal tracts to form the **Spinal Lemniscus**.
  • 3rd Order Synapses in the **Ventral Posterolateral (VPL) Thalamus**.
  • Cortex Projection to **Postcentral Gyrus** (Somatosensory Cortex) ‚Üí Contralateral, inverted body representation.

Types and Stimuli

  • Fast Pain: ~0.1 sec, sharp/pricking, mainly skin, via **A delta fibers** ‚Üí Lateral Spinothalamic Tract.
  • Slow Pain: ‚â•1 sec, burning/aching, all tissues, via **C fibers** ‚Üí Multiple relays in posterior gray horn.
  • Excitatory Chemicals: Serotonin, Histamine, Bradykinin, Lactic Acid, K ions. **Prostaglandins** and **Substance P** are threshold modulators.
  • Winding Up: Repeated C fiber stimulation ‚Üí **glutamate release** ‚Üí increased response (hyperalgesia).

Pain Control Mechanisms

  • Gating Theory:

    Nonpainful tactile input (massage/acupuncture) via large myelinated fibers excites connector neurons that **inhibit** pain fiber transmission ("closes the gate").

  • Analgesia System:

    Brainstem areas (Periventricular Diencephalon, Periaqueductal Gray) project via the **Reticulospinal Tract** to the posterior gray column.

    • Neurotransmitters: Enkephalins, Endorphins, and Serotonin **inhibit the release of Substance P**.

Flow of Signals (Light/Crude Touch & Pressure)

  • 1st Order Posterior root ganglion ‚Üí tip of posterior gray column ‚Üí Tract of Lissauer.
  • Synapse Synapses in the **Substantia Gelatinosa**.
  • 2nd Order Axons cross and ascend in the **Anterolateral White Column** as the Anterior Spinothalamic Tract.
  • Ascent Forms the **Spinal Lemniscus** with the Lateral Spinothalamic Tract.
  • 3rd Order Synapses in the **VPL Thalamus**.
  • Cortex Projection to **Postcentral Gyrus** ‚Üí **Crude awareness** of touch and pressure.
  • Somatotopic Order: Sacral fibers are **lateral**, cervical fibers are **medial** (same as Lateral Spinothalamic).

Fasciculi & Functions

  • Fasciculus Gracilis: Located **medially**. Carries fibers from **sacral, lumbar, and lower thoracic** regions (lower limb/trunk).
  • Fasciculus Cuneatus: Located **laterally**. Carries fibers from **upper thoracic and cervical** regions (upper limb/trunk).
  • Primary Functions: **Fine discriminative touch**, **two-point discrimination**, **vibratory sense**, and **conscious position sense** (proprioception).

Flow of Signals (Medial Lemniscal System)

  • 1st Order Posterior root ganglion ‚Üí Axons ascend **ipsilaterally** in the posterior white column.
  • Synapse Synapse in the **Nuclei Gracilis & Cuneatus** in the lower medulla.
  • 2nd Order Axons emerge as **Internal Arcuate Fibers** ‚Üí cross the midline ‚Üí ascend as the **Medial Lemniscus**.
  • 3rd Order Synapses in the **VPL Thalamus**.
  • Cortex Projection to **Postcentral Gyrus**.

Unconscious Proprioception

  • Posterior Spinocerebellar Tract:

    Axons from **Clarke‚Äôs column** ‚Üí ascends **ipsilaterally** in the lateral white column ‚Üí **Inferior Cerebellar Peduncle** ‚Üí Cerebellar Cortex. Info: trunk & lower limb muscle/joint sense.

  • Anterior Spinocerebellar Tract:

    Majority **cross** to the contralateral column; minority ipsilateral. Fibers reach cerebellum via the **Superior Cerebellar Peduncle**. **The crossed fibers recross in the cerebellum.** Info: trunk, upper/lower limbs, some skin.

  • Cuneocerebellar Tract:

    Fibers originating from the Accessory Cuneate Nucleus ‚Üí ascends **ipsilaterally** ‚Üí **Inferior Cerebellar Peduncle** ‚Üí Cerebellum. Function: **Upper limb** muscle-joint sense.

  • Spino-olivary Tract:

    Second-order neurons cross ‚Üí ascends ‚Üí terminates in the **Inferior Olivary Nuclei** ‚Üí **crosses again** ‚Üí Cerebellum. Function: cutaneous and proprioceptive info.

  • Spinotectal Tract:

    Ascent Crosses and ascends in the anterolateral white column ‚Üí terminates in the **Superior Colliculus** of the midbrain. Function: **Spinovisual reflexes** (eye and head movement toward a stimulus).

  • Spinoreticular Tract:

    Ascent Terminates in the **Reticular Formation** (medulla, pons, midbrain). Function: Provides afferent input for the general state of **consciousness** and basic reflexes.

  • Visceral Sensory Tracts:

    Path Thorax & abdomen sensory fibers enter posterior roots ‚Üí synapse in posterior/lateral gray columns ‚Üí join the **Spinothalamic Tract** ‚Üí VPL Thalamus ‚Üí Postcentral Gyrus. Many also mediate reflex activity.
