Meninges: Anatomy & Vasculature

Layers of Dura Mater

  • Endosteal layer:

    • Periosteum covering inner skull surface.
    • Not continuous with spinal dura at foramen magnum.
    • Strongly adherent at skull base.

  • Meningeal layer (Dura mater proper):

    • Dense, strong fibrous membrane covering brain.
    • Continuous with spinal dura through foramen magnum.
    • Forms tubular sheaths for cranial nerves → fuses with epineurium.

Dural Septa (Inward Folds) → Compartmentalization 🪢

  • Falx Cerebri:

    Midline fold between cerebral hemispheres.

    • Anterior: Internal frontal crest & crista galli.
    • Posterior: Blends with tentorium cerebelli.
    • Sinus: Superior sagittal (upper), Inferior sagittal (lower free), Straight (attachment to tentorium).

  • Tentorium Cerebelli:

    Crescent-shaped, roofs posterior cranial fossa, supports occipital lobes.

    • Notch: Tentorial notch → midbrain passage.
    • Fixed Border: Posterior clinoid, petrous bone, transverse sinus grooves.
    • Recess: For trigeminal nerve & ganglion (near petrous apex).
  • Falx Cerebelli: Small sickle-shaped fold between cerebellar hemispheres. Posterior fixed margin → occipital sinus.
  • Diaphragma Sellae: Circular fold forming sella turcica roof. Small central opening → hypophysis stalk.
  • Key Innervations: Trigeminal nerve branches, Vagus nerve, First three cervical spinal nerves, Sympathetic trunk branches.
  • Sensory Endings: Stretch-sensitive → headache.
  • Referred Pain (Above Tentorium): Pain referred to ipsilateral head skin.
  • Referred Pain (Below Tentorium): Pain referred to back of neck/scalp (via greater occipital nerve).

Main Source Arteries

Internal carotid, Maxillary, Ascending pharyngeal, Occipital, Vertebral.

Middle Meningeal Artery (Clinically Important)

  • Origin & Route: From maxillary artery → foramen spinosum → between dura layers. Runs forward & lateral on temporal bone.

  • Key Branches:

    • Anterior: Grooves anterior-inferior parietal bone → precentral gyrus region.
    • Posterior: Supplies posterior dura.
  • Meningeal Veins: Lie lateral to arteries, drain into pterygoid plexus or sphenoparietal sinus.

Located between dura layers, lined by endothelium, valveless. Receive cerebral veins & CSF → drain into internal jugular veins.

Key Sinuses Overview

  • Superior Sagittal Sinus: Upper falx cerebri → confluence of sinuses → usually right transverse sinus.
  • Inferior Sagittal Sinus: Lower falx cerebri → joins great cerebral vein → straight sinus.
  • Straight Sinus: Falx cerebri + tentorium junction → ends in transverse sinus.
  • Transverse Sinus: Paired, attached tentorium → turns into sigmoid sinus.
  • Sigmoid Sinus: Curves → jugular foramen → internal jugular vein.
  • Occipital Sinus: Falx cerebelli → communicates with vertebral veins → confluence.

Cavernous Sinus (Middle Cranial Fossa)

  • Interior: Trabeculated → spongy appearance.
  • Passes Inside: Internal carotid artery + Abducent nerve (CN VI).
  • Lateral Wall: CN III, IV, V1, V2.
  • Connections: Drains into petrosal sinuses & pterygoid venous plexus. Communicates via intercavernous sinuses.
  • Clinical Risk: Connection to facial vein → route for facial infections.

Delicate, impermeable membrane between dura & pia.

Key Spaces & Structures

  • Subdural Space: Potential space with fluid film.
  • Subarachnoid Space: Actual space → contains CSF, cerebral arteries, veins, cranial nerves.
  • Arachnoid Villi & Granulations: Project into dural sinuses → CSF absorption into bloodstream.

  • Cisternae (Large Subarachnoid Spaces):

    • Cisterna Cerebellomedullaris: Between cerebellum & 4th ventricle.
    • Cisterna Interpeduncularis: Between cerebral peduncles.
  • Nerve Sheaths: Fuses with epineurium; Optic nerve sheath → sclera.
  • Structure: Vascular, closely invests gyri & sulci.
  • Sheath Extension: Extends over cranial nerves → fuses with epineurium. Cerebral arteries carry pia sheath into brain substance.
  • Choroid Plexus: Forms tela choroidea in 3rd & 4th ventricles → fuses with ependyma → choroid plexus.

Production & Circulation

  • Production: Choroid plexus in lateral, 3rd, and 4th ventricles.
  • Exit: Ventricles → subarachnoid space via three foramina of 4th ventricle (median + two lateral).
  • Circulation: Over cerebral hemispheres, around spinal cord (down to S2).
  • Absorption: Arachnoid villi → dural sinuses → bloodstream.

Key Functions

  • Removes metabolic waste.
  • Brain flotation → protection from trauma.
  • Hormonal transport.
