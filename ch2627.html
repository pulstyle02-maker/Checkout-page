CHAPTER 26: URINE FORMATION (I)

Glomerular Filtration, Renal Blood Flow, and Their Control

Exhaustive reconstruction based on Guyton & Hall Textbook of Medical Physiology.

I. MULTIPLE FUNCTIONS OF THE KIDNEYS

The kidneys are not just for urine formation; they are the master regulators of homeostasis.

  • Excretion of Metabolic Waste: Urea (protein), Creatinine (muscle), Uric Acid (nucleic acids), Bilirubin (Hb), Hormone metabolites.
  • Excretion of Foreign Chemicals: Pesticides, drugs, toxins.
  • Regulation of Water & Electrolytes: Maintains homeostasis despite widely varying intake.
  • Arterial Pressure Regulation:
    • Long-term: Pressure Natriuresis (Na/H2O excretion).
    • Short-term: Renin secretion.
  • Acid-Base Balance: Excretes acids (sulfuric/phosphoric) & regulates Bicarbonate.
  • Erythrocyte Production: Secretes EPO in response to hypoxia.
  • Vitamin D Activation: Hydroxylates Vit D to Calcitriol (essential for Ca++ absorption).
  • Gluconeogenesis: Synthesizes glucose from amino acids during prolonged fasting.

II. PHYSIOLOGICAL ANATOMY

1. General Organization

  • Hilum: Medial entry/exit point.
  • Cortex/Medulla: Medulla contains 8-10 Pyramids ending in Papillae.
  • Drainage: Papilla ‚Üí Minor Calyx ‚Üí Major Calyx ‚Üí Renal Pelvis ‚Üí Ureter.

2. Renal Blood Supply (22% of Cardiac Output)

Pathway: Renal A. ‚Üí Interlobar ‚Üí Arcuate ‚Üí Interlobular ‚Üí Afferent Arteriole ‚Üí Glomerulus (60 mmHg) ‚Üí Efferent Arteriole ‚Üí Peritubular Caps (13 mmHg) ‚Üí Venous System.

Note the two capillary beds in series: High pressure glomerular (filtration) and low pressure peritubular (reabsorption).

3. The Nephron

  • ~1 million per kidney. No regeneration. Loss of 10% per decade after age 40.
  • Cortical Nephrons (70-80%): Short loops, peritubular capillaries.
  • Juxtamedullary Nephrons (20-30%): Deep glomeruli, long loops dipping into papilla, Vasa Recta blood supply. Crucial for urine concentration.

III. MICTURITION

  • Detrusor: Smooth muscle syncytium (gap junctions). 40-60 mmHg pressure.
  • Innervation:
    • Parasympathetic (S2-S3): Pelvic Nerves. Motor to Detrusor. *Main emptying nerve*.
    • Sympathetic (T11-L2): Hypogastric Nerves. Inhibits Detrusor, constricts Internal Sphincter (Storage).
    • Somatic (S2-S4): Pudendal Nerve. Voluntary control of External Sphincter.
  • Reflex: Self-regenerative stretch reflex. Facilitated or Inhibited by Pons (Brainstem) and Cortex.

IV. URINE FORMATION FORMULA

Excretion = Filtration - Reabsorption + Secretion

  • Creatinine: Filtered only.
  • Electrolytes: Filtered + Partially Reabsorbed.
  • Glucose: Filtered + 100% Reabsorbed.
  • Acids/Drugs: Filtered + Secreted.

V. GFR & GLOMERULAR MEMBRANE

  • Normal GFR: 125 ml/min (180 L/day).
  • Filtration Fraction: GFR/RPF = 0.2 (20%).
  • Membrane Layers: Endothelium (fenestrated), Basement Membrane (negative charge), Podocytes (slit pores).
  • Selectivity: Barrier to Proteins due to Size and Negative Charge. (Minimal Change Disease = loss of charge = proteinuria).

VI. DETERMINANTS OF GFR

GFR = Kf √ó Net Filtration Pressure

  • Forces Favoring: Glomerular Hydrostatic Pressure (PG) = 60 mmHg.
  • Forces Opposing: Bowman's Hydrostatic (PB) = 18 mmHg + Glomerular Oncotic (œÄG) = 32 mmHg.
  • Net Pressure: +10 mmHg.
  • Kf: 12.5 ml/min/mmHg. Reduced in Diabetes/Hypertension.

VII. PHYSIOLOGICAL CONTROL

  • Sympathetic: Constricts Afferent/Efferent. Decreases GFR (only in severe stress).
  • Angiotensin II: Constricts Efferent. Maintains GFR in hypotension.
  • Prostaglandins/NO: Dilate Afferent. Protect GFR. (NSAIDs block this ‚Üí renal failure).

VIII. AUTOREGULATION (75-160 mmHg)

  • Tubuloglomerular Feedback: Macula Densa senses low NaCl ‚Üí Dilates Afferent + Stimulates Renin (Ang II constricts Efferent) ‚Üí Restores GFR.
  • Myogenic: Stretch of arteriole causes contraction.
