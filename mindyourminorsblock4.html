Gastritis Pathology MCQs

Practice App for Medical / Pathology Students

1. Gastritis means inflammation of which layer of the stomach?

Correct answer: C. Gastric mucosa
Gastritis is inflammation of the stomach mucosal lining.

2. The most common cause of chronic gastritis is:

Correct answer: A. Helicobacter pylori infection
H. pylori is the major cause of chronic active gastritis.

3. Acute gastritis is commonly associated with:

Correct answer: A. NSAID use
NSAIDs reduce prostaglandin synthesis, weakening the gastric mucosal barrier.

4. Which inflammatory cell is commonly seen in acute gastritis?

Correct answer: A. Neutrophils
Acute inflammation is mainly characterized by neutrophilic infiltration.

5. Autoimmune gastritis most commonly affects the:

Correct answer: B. Fundus and body
Autoimmune gastritis damages parietal cells mainly in the body and fundus.

6. Autoimmune gastritis may lead to deficiency of:

Correct answer: B. Vitamin B12
Destruction of parietal cells decreases intrinsic factor, causing vitamin B12 deficiency.

7. Pernicious anemia is associated with:

Correct answer: B. Autoimmune gastritis
Autoimmune destruction of parietal cells causes intrinsic factor deficiency and pernicious anemia.

8. H. pylori mainly colonizes the:

Correct answer: A. Gastric mucus layer
H. pylori lives in the mucus layer overlying gastric epithelium.

9. Which enzyme helps H. pylori survive in acidic stomach environment?

Correct answer: B. Urease
Urease breaks down urea into ammonia, which neutralizes gastric acid around the bacteria.

10. Chronic gastritis can increase the risk of:

Correct answer: A. Gastric carcinoma
Long-standing chronic gastritis may cause atrophy, intestinal metaplasia, dysplasia, and carcinoma.
